Should France legalize Cannabis?

Disclaimer

A century long story comes from drugs consumption. It could be considered as a history tag, a counter-culture symbol, even a war weapon and sometimes as a therapeutic treatment. If its bad boy reputation is well known, the society consumes, restrains and perceives its consumption in a complex way. Between health risks and money potentially earned by a regulated market, the states nowadays have to think about its legalization. As Canada did recently, does France have to rethink the legal status of marijuana?

Cannabis legal status around the world
Inspired by american way of life

We’ll start our data journey in the United States, first power of in the world. According to the latest surveys, 10 % of the US citizens consider themselves as active marijuana consumers . As a meaningful comparison, 10% of the US population (30M) represents the entire Canadian population, which recently passed a new law to legalize the recreative use of marijuana.

Who are the smokers ?

In terms of sociology of marijuana consumption, we first wanted to determine the standard profil of a user. We tried to identify tendencies based on your gender, ethnic origins or even work status. However, our data driven analysis didn’t lead us to a clear identification.
We notice that men consume slightly more than women but the difference is not strong enough to make it a generalization. Finally, the cultural analysis only proves that the marijuana use is a global concern and is not impacted by your ethnic origins.

Economical impact

If marijuana is considered as harmful and responsible for illegal traffic in many countries, its regulation has proven to bring wealth and employment in states that have legalized it. Our example comes here from Denver, Colorado, where 18,000 jobs have been created statewide in 2015 thanks to the marijuana legalization (directly and indirectly created as well as induced). Compared to other “legal drugs” such as cigarettes and alcohol, Marijuana is still a bit behind in 2015, but is expected to be the first expense among the three in 2020 ($150 millions) As for the revenue for the state, we see that taxes (retail,sales taxes and fees) are bringing around 250 millions of dollars a year , representing a little more than 2% of the total general fund revenue of the state in 2018

Is France missing an opportunity ?

In the light of concrete cases, taking into account the french population’s opinion on marijuana and putting in perspective with other addictive substances that are legal, it is today fair to wonder if there is still a good reason to keep marijuana illegal in France.

Zoom on France
- Criminality -

France, considering its geographical position at the heart of the Western Europe, is a zone of transit for many illicit substances produced in the world. Cannabis is one of those substances but it counts for 90% of the drug related criminal cases. Let's take a look at the statistics !

Zoom on France
- Public Health -

Cannabis is the 8th most dangerous drug . This ranking take into account 16 parameters including: direct effects on user, overdose risk, physical and psychological dependancy, alteration of mental capacities but also the economic cost for a country and the international smuggling consequences. This outputs two scores: one for the harm the drug can inflict to the user but also to others
